01

Overview

Gmail Unsubscriber (the "Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that helps you remove yourself from unwanted email mailing lists. When you provide a list of sender email addresses and initiate a run, the Extension accesses your Gmail account solely to locate unsubscribe links within those emails and execute the unsubscribe action on your behalf.

Plain-language summary: This Extension reads your emails only to find unsubscribe links. It does not store, transmit, sell, or share your email content or personal data with anyone. Everything runs locally in your browser.
02

What Data We Access

To perform its function, the Extension accesses the following data from your Gmail account, strictly on-demand and only when you initiate a run:

Email metadata

The Extension searches your inbox for emails matching the sender addresses you provide. It retrieves the message ID and basic metadata (sender, subject, date) of the most recent email from each sender.

Email headers

The Extension reads the List-Unsubscribe and List-Unsubscribe-Post headers of retrieved messages. These are technical fields added by mailing list software that contain unsubscribe URLs.

Email body (when necessary)

If no List-Unsubscribe header is present, the Extension reads the HTML and plain-text body of the email solely to locate an unsubscribe link using pattern matching. No body content is stored or transmitted.

OAuth access token

Chrome's built-in identity system provides a short-lived OAuth token to authenticate requests to the Gmail API. This token is managed entirely by Chrome and is never stored by the Extension.

03

What We Do Not Collect

  • We do not read, store, or transmit the content of your emails beyond locating unsubscribe links.
  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any other personal identifiers.
  • We do not track your browsing history or activity outside of the unsubscribe pages visited during a run.
  • We do not send any data to external servers. There is no backend server associated with this Extension.
  • We do not use analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or any third-party tracking SDKs.
  • We do not sell, license, or share any data with third parties.
  • We do not use your data to train machine learning models.
04

How Data Is Used

All data accessed by the Extension is used for one purpose only: to locate and execute the unsubscribe action for sender addresses you explicitly provide. Specifically:

  1. Your list of sender addresses is used to query the Gmail API for matching messages.
  2. Email headers and body content are scanned in memory to extract unsubscribe URLs.
  3. Extracted URLs are navigated to in temporary background browser tabs.
  4. The Extension attempts to click the unsubscribe button on those pages.
  5. Background tabs are closed immediately after the action completes.

No step in this process involves transmitting data outside your local browser environment.

05

Data Storage & Retention

The Extension stores only the following minimal data locally in Chrome's extension storage (chrome.storage.local):

  • Run summary: After a run completes, the total count of senders processed, succeeded, and failed, along with a timestamp. No sender addresses or email content are stored in this summary.
  • In-progress updates: If the popup is closed mid-run, the most recent progress event is temporarily cached so the popup can restore its state when reopened. This is overwritten on every update and cleared at the end of each run.

This data is stored locally on your device only, is never transmitted anywhere, and can be cleared at any time by removing the Extension from Chrome.

06

Third-Party Services

Google Gmail API

The Extension uses the Google Gmail API to search for and retrieve emails. Your use of the Gmail API is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. The Extension requests only the minimum necessary Gmail scopes: read access and the ability to modify labels (used solely to support future trash functionality if enabled).

Unsubscribe landing pages

When the Extension navigates to an unsubscribe URL, you are briefly visiting a third-party website (e.g., the newsletter platform that sent the email). Those websites have their own privacy policies and may log the visit. The Extension does not control, and is not responsible for, the privacy practices of those sites.

No other third parties

The Extension does not integrate with any analytics services, advertising networks, data brokers, or any other third-party platforms.

07

Chrome Permissions Explained

The Extension requests the following Chrome permissions, each limited to what is strictly necessary:

  • identity — Authenticates your Google account via OAuth so the Extension can access the Gmail API on your behalf.
  • tabs — Opens unsubscribe pages in temporary background tabs and closes them automatically after use.
  • scripting — Injects JavaScript into unsubscribe landing pages to locate and click the unsubscribe button.
  • storage — Saves run summaries and temporary progress state locally on your device.
  • alarms — Keeps the background service worker active during long-running jobs to prevent Chrome from terminating it prematurely.
  • Host permissions (https://*/*) — Required to visit unsubscribe pages, which can be hosted on any domain. Only URLs sourced directly from your own Gmail messages are ever visited.
08

Your Rights & Controls

You retain full control over the Extension's access to your data at all times:

  • Revoke access: You can revoke the Extension's Gmail access at any time by visiting Google Account Permissions and removing the Extension's authorization.
  • Sign out: Use the "Sign out" button within the Extension popup to immediately invalidate the cached OAuth token.
  • Remove the Extension: Uninstalling the Extension from Chrome immediately deletes all locally stored data, including run history and any cached state.
  • Nothing runs automatically: The Extension never accesses your Gmail account in the background. It only acts when you explicitly click the Start button.
09

Children's Privacy

This Extension is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect any personal information from children. The Extension is intended for use by adults managing their own email accounts.
